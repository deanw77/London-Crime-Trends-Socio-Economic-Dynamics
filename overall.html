London Crime Trends


We first take a brief look at the distribution of crime across the London Boroughs


We can see that central London is a hotspot for crime. This largely due to much higher rates of theft in central London. In fact theft Accounts for over 50% of all crime in Central London. The next most common crime is violence against the person, which accounts for around 20% of all crime in Central London.


If we remove theft from the data we get a more even distribution of crime across the boroughs. This is because theft is much more common in central London, where there are more people and more opportunities for theft. In contrast, violence against the person is more evenly distributed across the boroughs.

2012

In August 2011, London experienced a significant crime spike and widespread civil unrest, including riots that involved looting, arson, and violence. The unrest was triggered by the police shooting of Mark Duggan. According to the Royal Economic Society and the Home Office in the affected areas, crime rose by 57% during August.

2019

In 2019, the British Transport Police released an online reporting tool. This led to increase in recorded crime but not necesarily reflecting an increase in crime, as incidents that usually would not have been reported were now being recorded.

Theft Drop

Phone technology, including features like activation lock, theft detection lock, and remote tracking, is playing a role in reducing street theft by making stolen phones less desirable to thieves. Along with the fact less people carry cash, street robbery is falling
